A unified model for zero-shot singing voice conversion and synthesis

Jui-Te Wu, Jun-You Wang, Jyh-Shing Roger Jang, and Li Su

[ISMIR 2022 poster & paper]

in Proc. of the 23rd Int. Society for Music Information Retrieval Conf., Bengaluru, India, 2022.

This is the static demo website of the paper "A unified model for zero-shot singing voice conversion and synthesis", which will be presented in the ISMIR 2022 conference.

In this paper, we propose a unified model for zero-shot singing voice synthesis (SVS) and singing voice conversion (SVC). By going one step further from Bonada and Blaauw's previous work on semi-supervised SVS (this paper), we have demonstrated the possibility of achieving zero-shot SVS and SVC, all in a same model, with the use of both labeled and unlabeled singing data for model training.

Unseen SVC

Demo songs synthesized in the unseen SVC scenario, where a source audio and a target audio are taken as input. The model synthesizes the audio which contains both the singing content from the source audio and the singer identity from the target audio. Both the singers of the source audio and target audio were unseen during model training.

Unseen SVS

Demo songs synthesized in the unseen SVS scenario, where the frame-level phoneme information, pitch contour, and a target audio are taken as input. The model synthesizes the audio which contains both the singing content from the phoneme and pitch contour, and the singer identity from the target audio. The phoneme information and pitch contour are extracted/aligned from the source audio. The singer of the target audio was unseen during model training.
